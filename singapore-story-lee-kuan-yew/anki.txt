What period does "The Singapore Story" cover and what are the three great struggles?	1923-1965 (birth through independence). Three struggles: against colonialism (ending British rule), against communism (defeating infiltration of the independence movement), and against communalism (fighting racial/religious extremism).	book::singapore-story chapter::overview concept::three-struggles type::definition
How did the Japanese occupation shape Lee Kuan Yew's political worldview?	It shattered the myth of British invincibility, taught him that power (not law) was the ultimate arbiter, and convinced him only self-governance could protect a people. He narrowly survived the sook ching massacre by sensing danger and escaping a screening line.	book::singapore-story chapter::occupation concept::formative-trauma type::application
What was the PAP's "united front" strategy and why was it dangerous?	A deliberate alliance between Lee's English-educated moderates and Chinese-educated communists. Lee needed their mass base; they needed his legal front. The gamble: ride the communist tiger to power without being devoured. He later dismantled them through Operation Coldstore.	book::singapore-story chapter::pap-founding concept::united-front type::definition
How badly did the PAP split damage Lee's party?	35 of 51 branch committees and 19 of 23 paid organizers defected to the communist Barisan Sosialis in 1961. The PAP nearly collapsed. Lee rebuilt by using Operation Coldstore (113 detained) and winning the 1963 election.	book::singapore-story chapter::split concept::barisan-sosialis type::application
Why was Singapore expelled from Malaysia?	Lee's "Malaysian Malaysia" (race-blind meritocracy) directly threatened UMNO's policy of Malay supremacy. After the 1964 racial riots (36 dead), the Tunku concluded expulsion was the only solution. Parliament voted 126-0 for separation on August 9, 1965.	book::singapore-story chapter::separation concept::expulsion type::definition
Why did Lee Kuan Yew cry on television on August 9, 1965?	He was not celebrating independence — he was mourning the failure of merger with Malaysia, which he believed was Singapore's best hope for survival. Independence was forced on Singapore, not chosen. His deputy Goh Keng Swee was more decisive about the break.	book::singapore-story chapter::separation concept::tearful-press-conference type::application
What was Lee's approach to anti-corruption and why did it work?	Zero tolerance from 1959: strengthened CPIB, enacted Prevention of Corruption Act (1960), paid competitive civil service salaries to remove financial incentive, prosecuted PAP members when caught. System-based, not personality-based — it survived beyond Lee.	book::singapore-story chapter::governance concept::anti-corruption type::definition
Why did Lee choose English as Singapore's common working language?	It was ethnically neutral (no race could claim advantage), connected Singapore to global commerce, and prevented any single ethnic group from dominating. Four official languages (Malay, Mandarin, Tamil, English) preserved cultural identity alongside the common tongue.	book::singapore-story chapter::governance concept::language-policy type::application
What does "every other policy has to bend at the knees for our water survival" mean?	After Japan cut water pipes (1942) and Malaysia threatened supply (1965), Lee made water self-sufficiency a 50-year national priority. Existential resources cannot depend on foreign goodwill. Led to NEWater and desalination programs.	book::singapore-story chapter::survival concept::water-independence type::definition
What is "Designed Coexistence" as a governing philosophy?	In multi-ethnic societies, goodwill alone is insufficient. Integration must be structurally enforced: housing quotas preventing ethnic enclaves, common working language, guaranteed minority parliamentary representation. The 1964 riots proved natural harmony doesn't emerge by itself.	book::singapore-story chapter::governance concept::designed-coexistence type::definition
Who was Goh Keng Swee and what was his key contribution?	Singapore's economic architect. First Finance Minister, later Defense Minister. Met Lee at Cambridge's Malayan Forum. More decisive than Lee on separation — pushed for a clean break while Lee wavered. Designed the economic strategy and built the armed forces.	book::singapore-story chapter::key-figures concept::goh-keng-swee type::definition
What distinguished Lee from other postcolonial leaders?	Most built governance around charisma and ideology. Lee built institutions: CPIB, competitive civil service pay, bilingual education, ethnic housing quotas. He pragmatically adopted whatever worked regardless of ideological origin. Systems that outlive their founders.	book::singapore-story chapter::governance concept::institutional-design type::comparison
Why did Lee write this memoir at age 75?	Fear of complacency. "I was troubled by the apparent over-confidence of a generation that has only known stability, growth and prosperity." He wanted successors to understand Singapore's success was neither natural nor inevitable.	book::singapore-story chapter::epilogue concept::generational-warning type::application
What was Operation Coldstore and why is it controversial?	February 2, 1963: 113 people detained without trial — Barisan Sosialis leaders, unionists, activists. Lee's justification: they were communist operatives. Critics: it was politically motivated to eliminate opposition before the 1963 election. The Barisan never recovered.	book::singapore-story chapter::coldstore concept::detention-without-trial type::definition
Who was Kwa Geok Choo and what role did she play?	Lee's wife and intellectual equal. Cambridge law graduate who often outperformed him academically. Co-founded Lee & Lee law firm, helped draft the PAP constitution, and was a founding party member. A Peranakan woman "often the smarter one in the room."	book::singapore-story chapter::personal concept::kwa-geok-choo type::definition
