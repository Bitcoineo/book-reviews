What is "skin in the game" in Taleb's framework?	Having personal exposure to the consequences of your decisions. If you capture the upside, you must also bear the downside. When this symmetry breaks, systems become fragile and unethical.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::prologue concept::sitg type::definition
What is the Bob Rubin Trade?	An arrangement where one party captures gains while transferring losses to others. Named after Robert Rubin who opposed regulating derivatives as Treasury Secretary, then collected $120M+ from Citigroup. When the 2008 crisis hit, taxpayers paid — not Rubin.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::prologue concept::bob-rubin-trade type::definition
Why does bailing out banks after a crisis make the next crisis more likely?	Bailouts remove skin in the game. If bankers know they'll be rescued, they're incentivized to take even larger risks — they keep the gains in good years, and taxpayers absorb the losses in bad years. The asymmetry rewards recklessness.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::1 concept::moral-hazard type::application
What is the Minority Rule?	An intransigent minority (as small as 3-4%) can impose its preferences on a flexible majority because the cost of compliance is asymmetric. The majority doesn't care enough to resist; the minority refuses to budge.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::2 concept::minority-rule type::definition
How does the Minority Rule explain why entire food supply chains go kosher?	If one family member requires kosher food, the whole family eats kosher (easier than two meals). At scale, a small percentage of strict kosher consumers forces producers to certify everything — the flexible majority accepts it without noticing.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::2 concept::minority-rule type::application
Who is the "Intellectual Yet Idiot" (IYI)?	A credentialed person who tells others what to do without bearing consequences for being wrong. They confuse theory with practice. They were wrong on Iraq, Libya, financial models — but never paid for their errors, so they never learned.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::6 concept::iyi type::definition
Why might a street-smart trader outperform a Nobel-winning economist in real markets?	The trader has skin in the game — being wrong means losing money. The economist faces no personal cost for bad predictions. Skin in the game forces practical learning that theory alone cannot provide. "People have two brains — one with skin, one without."	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::6 concept::iyi type::application
What is the Lindy Effect?	For non-perishable things (ideas, books, technologies), future life expectancy is proportional to current age. A book in print for 100 years will likely last another 100. Time is the ultimate quality filter. Applies to ideas, not biological organisms.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::8 concept::lindy-effect type::definition
Why does Taleb prefer the Silver Rule over the Golden Rule?	The Golden Rule ("do unto others...") is too prescriptive — it enables busybodies forcing their vision of good onto others. The Silver Rule ("do NOT do unto others...") prevents meddling and respects autonomy. Removing harm is more robust than imposing help.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::prologue concept::silver-rule type::comparison
Why does Taleb say "choose the surgeon who looks like a butcher"?	Someone who doesn't look the part had to overcome far more resistance to reach that position. Their actual competence must be superior to compensate for the appearance disadvantage. Looking the part may mean you were selected for appearance, not skill.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::9 concept::counter-signaling type::application
What is the Green Lumber Fallacy?	A trader made a fortune selling "green lumber" while believing it was lumber painted green (it's freshly cut wood). A trader who knew every technical detail went bankrupt. Practical knowledge with skin in the game beats theoretical knowledge without it.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::prologue concept::green-lumber type::application
What does "pathemata mathemata" mean?	Greek for "learning through pain." Real learning only happens when you bear consequences for being wrong. Risk-free advice, forecasting, and policy-making produce garbage because the feedback loop is broken.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::prologue concept::pathemata-mathemata type::definition
How does Hammurabi's Code relate to skin in the game?	3,800 years ago: "If a builder builds a house that collapses and kills the owner, the builder shall be put to death." It establishes symmetry — you bear the downside of your work. Taleb argues this ancient heuristic is more effective than modern regulation.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::prologue concept::hammurabi type::application
What does "soul in the game" mean beyond "skin in the game"?	Soul in the game means sacrificing your own well-being for something larger — saints, artisans, true entrepreneurs. They give more than they take because it is their mission. An artisan obsessed with craft quality has soul, not just skin.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::prologue concept::soul-in-the-game type::definition
What is Taleb's view on ergodicity and why does it matter for risk?	Most real-world situations are non-ergodic: one person's path over time does NOT match the average of many people at one moment. If there's any possibility of total ruin (going to zero), expected-value calculations are meaningless. Ruin must be treated separately.	book::skin-in-the-game chapter::19 concept::ergodicity type::definition
